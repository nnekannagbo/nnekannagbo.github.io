VOICE DESIGN

CRA Bot — Designing a voice assistant to help taxpayers mail forms

February 2020

I worked as the lead Designer on a multidisciplinary team to solve a complex, cross-channel business problem for the Government of Canada. For over 3 months, we conducted interviews, research, and prototyped, tested, and developed a conversational voice bot, powered through Amazon Lex, to help Canadians find the correct mailing address for their tax forms.

Role

  • Design Lead, UX research, conversation design, voice UI, logic flow
  • Agile design process and Design thinking - Worked alongside a dedicated front-end developer in San Francisco, and with the foundational team based in Israel with the Canada School of Public Service's Digital Academy
  • Fellowship duration: 3 months (1 week Bootcamp, 6 weeks of training, 2.5 weeks of Practicum)

Project goal

Canadians face challenges when mailing their documents to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). Many Canadians don‚Äôt know which address to mail their documents and completed forms to. As a result, some Canadians resort to calling the CRA call centres for support, or they may send their mail to the wrong address which delays processing for Canadians and increases work for mailroom staff.

Help Canadians find the correct mailing address for their CRA-related forms and documents.

Mailing forms to the CRA is a cross-channel problem. It impacts Canadians as well as internal CRA staff in the mailrooms and call centre agents. It also costs the CRA money for every call that they receive. We wanted to find a way to make form submission simpler and diverse for Canadians.

Identifying our users

The user base for this project consists of people in the 18-30 age range. The main requirements were transparency and friendliness. Univi needed to be easily understandable by people without any specific knowledge regarding investment or saving strategies.

How might we create a system that is inclusive, iterative, and responsive to help CRA clients (Canadians, taxpayers) find the correct mailing address for their forms and documents?

We identified the 6 user groups that we felt were the most impacted by this problem and would benefit the most from a solution:

  • Canadians, the public
  • Businesses
  • Non-residents
  • Internal mailroom staff
  • Newcomers to Canada
  • ...

Closing the gaps

We interviewed various users and stakeholders over several weeks in attempt to understand their pain points with the current system and flesh out any future desires.

Patterns were beginning to emerge as we identified some similarities in terms of interviewee comments, pains, and gains. The interviews and affinity mapping revealed some new areas for us to address in our solution:

  • Need to accommodate newcomers to Canada.
  • Not all forms are mailed based on taxpayers location but rather to a tax centre that owns that workload. Our solution needs to account for complex mailing addresses.
